Data Sources

The Zoned Development Capacity Layers 2016, managed by a Private Member of the City of Seattle's ArcGIS Online, form a crucial part of the Zoned Development Capacity Model established in 2016. This comprehensive dataset includes the input and output layers necessary for evaluating the city's developmental capacity. The models primary function is to assess potential urban development by comparing current infrastructure to what could be built under the existing zoning laws. This comparative analysis provides insights into the possible expansion of residential and commercial projects within the city's precincts. A detailed summary of these findings supports the Seattle 2035 initiative, offering valuable resources, including reports, methodologies, and additional data exploration options.
